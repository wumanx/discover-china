Overview

West Lake (西湖, Xīhú) is the crown jewel of Chinese landscape design — a place where nature and art have been harmoniously intertwined for over a thousand years. Located in Hangzhou, just 180 km southwest of Shanghai, this UNESCO World Heritage Site has inspired countless poets, painters, and philosophers since the Tang Dynasty.

The lake is surrounded by hills on three sides, with the city of Hangzhou to the east. Its beauty is not just natural but deeply cultural — every bridge, pagoda, and causeway carries centuries of literary and artistic significance. The famous Song Dynasty poet Su Shi wrote: "Comparing West Lake to Xi Shi, whether lightly powdered or richly adorned, she is always beautiful."

🇯🇵 West Lake & Japan

West Lake's influence extends deeply into Japanese culture. During the Heian and Kamakura periods, Japanese monks and scholars visited Hangzhou and brought back the aesthetic ideals of West Lake garden design. Many famous Japanese gardens — including those in Kyoto — were directly inspired by West Lake's "borrowed scenery" technique (借景). The Japanese haiku tradition also shares roots with the nature poetry born on West Lake's shores.

The Ten Classic Scenes

Since the Southern Song Dynasty, poets have cataloged ten iconic views of West Lake. Each captures a unique moment of beauty:

1. Dawn on the Su Causeway in Spring (苏堤春晓)

A 2.8 km causeway lined with peach and willow trees, built by poet-governor Su Shi in 1089. Walk it at dawn in spring for an ethereal experience.

2. Curved Yard and Lotus Pool in Summer (曲院风荷)

Vast lotus ponds blooming in summer, with pavilions for viewing. The fragrance of lotus on a summer breeze is unforgettable.

3. Moon over the Peaceful Lake in Autumn (平湖秋月)

On a clear autumn night, the moon's reflection on the still lake creates a scene of perfect tranquility. Best viewed from the lakeside pavilion.

4. Lingering Snow on the Broken Bridge in Winter (断桥残雪)

When snow partially melts on the Broken Bridge, it appears "broken" from a distance — a scene of haunting beauty. Also famous as the meeting place in the Legend of the White Snake.

5. Leifeng Pagoda in Evening Glow (雷峰夕照)

The reconstructed pagoda on Sunset Hill, glowing in the evening sun. Connected to one of China's Four Great Folktales — the Legend of the White Snake.

Must-Do Experiences

  • Boat ride on the lake: Traditional rowboats (¥150/hour) or electric boats (¥50/person) — the only way to fully appreciate the landscape
  • Bicycle around the lake: The 15 km circuit takes about 2 hours, with bike rental stations everywhere
  • Longjing tea tasting: Visit the Dragon Well village for authentic Longjing (Dragon Well) green tea — China's most famous tea
  • Evening fountain show: The musical fountain near Hubin Road creates a spectacular water and light show
  • Impression West Lake: An open-air performance directed by Zhang Yimou, staged on the lake itself — breathtaking

Nearby Attractions

  • Lingyin Temple: One of China's oldest and most significant Buddhist temples (founded 326 AD)
  • Feilai Peak: Remarkable Buddhist cave carvings dating to the 10th century
  • Silk Museum: Hangzhou is China's silk capital — learn the 5,000-year history of Chinese silk
  • Hefang Street: Traditional shopping street with local snacks, silk products, and handicrafts

Practical Information

Getting There

From Shanghai: High-speed train from Shanghai Hongqiao to Hangzhou East (1 hour, ¥73). Then take Metro Line 1 to Longxiangqiao Station for the lake.

Best Seasons

  • Spring (March–May): Blossoms along the Su Causeway — the most popular time
  • Summer (June–August): Lotus flowers in full bloom
  • Autumn (September–November): Osmanthus fragrance fills the air
  • Winter (December–February): Snow scenes — the least crowded, most poetic time

Tips for Japanese Visitors

  • West Lake is free to visit — no entrance fee for the lake area
  • Individual attractions (Leifeng Pagoda ¥40, Lingyin Temple ¥75) charge separately
  • Book "Impression West Lake" tickets in advance (¥260–580)
  • Hangzhou is one of China's cleanest and greenest cities — very walkable
  • Try local specialties: West Lake vinegar fish, Dongpo pork, beggar's chicken
  • A day trip from Shanghai is possible, but two days allow a more relaxed pace
